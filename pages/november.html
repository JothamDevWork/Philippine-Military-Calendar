ALL SAINTS' DAY

November 1 is celebrated in the Philippines as All Saints' Day. It is a Christian holiday that honors all saints, known and unknown. In the Philippines, it is also a time when families remember and pray
for their departed loved ones.

Why is it important?

  • It honors the lives of saints who served as examples of faith.
  • It is a special time to remember deceased family members.
  • It reflects Filipino values of family unity and remembrance.

How do people observe it?

  • Visiting cemeteries and cleaning family graves.
  • Offering flowers, candles, and prayers.
  • Holding family gatherings at memorial sites.
  • Attending Mass in remembrance of loved ones.

All Saints' Day is a meaningful celebration of
faith, remembrance, and family in the Philippines.

ALL SOULS' DAY

November 2 is observed in the Philippines as All Souls' Day. It is a day dedicated to praying for the souls of the faithful departed. While November 1 honors the saints, this day focuses especially on remembering deceased family members and loved ones.

Why is it important?

  • It is a time to pray for the souls of the departed.
  • It strengthens family bonds through remembrance.
  • It reflects Filipino traditions of respect for ancestors.

How do people observe it?

  • Visiting cemeteries to offer prayers and light candles.
  • Bringing flowers and cleaning grave sites.
  • Attending Mass dedicated to the departed.
  • Spending time with family in remembrance.

All Souls' Day is a solemn day of prayer, remembrance, and love for departed loved ones in the Philippines.

BONIFACIO DAY

November 30 is celebrated in the Philippines as Bonifacio Day. It honors Andrés Bonifacio, the founder of the Katipunan and one of the country’s national heroes who led the revolution against Spanish colonial rule.

Why is it important?

  • It recognizes the bravery and leadership of Andrés Bonifacio.
  • It commemorates the Philippine Revolution against Spain.
  • It promotes patriotism and love for the country.

How do people observe it?

  • Holding flag-raising and wreath-laying ceremonies.
  • Visiting monuments dedicated to Bonifacio.
  • Conducting educational programs about his life and legacy.
  • Reflecting on the importance of courage and nationalism.

Bonifacio Day is a tribute to bravery, revolution,
and the enduring spirit of Filipino freedom.
