FEAST OF THE IMMACULATE CONCEPTION OF MARY

December 8 is celebrated in the Philippines as the Feast of the Immaculate Conception of Mary. It honors the Blessed Virgin Mary’s conception without original sin. She is the principal patroness of the Philippines.

Why is it important?

  • It honors the Blessed Virgin Mary as the Mother of Jesus.
  • It celebrates her purity and special role in salvation history.
  • It reflects the deep Marian devotion of Filipino Catholics.

How do people observe it?

  • Attending special Masses and church celebrations.
  • Holding Marian processions and prayers.
  • Offering flowers and lighting candles in honor of Mary.
  • Participating in religious programs in schools and communities.

The Feast of the Immaculate Conception is a
celebration of faith, purity, and devotion in the Philippines.

CHRISTMAS EVE

December 24 is celebrated as Christmas Eve in the Philippines. It is the final day of preparation before Christmas Day and is marked by family gatherings, festive meals, and midnight Mass known as "Simbang Gabi" or "Misa de Gallo."

Why is it important?

  • It celebrates the anticipation of the birth of Jesus Christ.
  • It brings families together for reunion and thanksgiving.
  • It reflects Filipino traditions and strong Christian faith.

How do people observe it?

  • Attending Midnight Mass (Misa de Gallo).
  • Sharing Noche Buena, a traditional midnight feast.
  • Exchanging gifts with family members.
  • Decorating homes with parols and Christmas lights.

Christmas Eve is a joyful celebration of faith, family,
and the coming of Christ.

CHRISTMAS DAY

December 25 is celebrated in the Philippines as Christmas Day. It commemorates the birth of Jesus Christ and is one of the most important and joyful Christian celebrations of the year. The Philippines is known for having one of the longest Christmas seasons in the world.

Why is it important?

  • It celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ, the Savior.
  • It highlights the values of love, generosity, and hope.
  • It strengthens family unity through shared traditions.

How do people observe it?

  • Attending Simbang Gabi and Christmas Day Mass.
  • Holding Noche Buena (midnight feast) with family.
  • Exchanging gifts and sharing meals.
  • Decorating homes with Christmas trees and parols.

Christmas Day is a joyful celebration of faith, love,
and togetherness in the Philippines.

RIZAL DAY

December 30 is celebrated in the Philippines as Rizal Day. It commemorates the martyrdom of Dr. José Rizal, the country's national hero, who was executed in 1896 at Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park) for inspiring the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule.

Why is it important?

  • It honors the life and sacrifices of Dr. José Rizal.
  • It remembers his role in awakening Filipino nationalism.
  • It promotes patriotism, education, and love of country.

How do people observe it?

  • Holding flag-raising and wreath-laying ceremonies.
  • Visiting Rizal Park and monuments dedicated to him.
  • Conducting programs and school activities about his life and works.
  • Reading his novels such as Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo.

Rizal Day is a solemn tribute to sacrifice, patriotism,
and the enduring fight for Filipino freedom.

NEW YEAR'S EVE

December 31 is celebrated in the Philippines as New Year's Eve. It marks the final day of the year and is a joyful time when families gather to welcome the coming year with hope and positivity.

Why is it important?

  • It symbolizes new beginnings and fresh opportunities.
  • It brings families together in celebration.
  • It reflects traditions meant to invite good luck and prosperity.

How do people observe it?

  • Preparing a Media Noche (midnight feast) with family.
  • Displaying 12 round fruits to symbolize prosperity.
  • Lighting fireworks and making noise at midnight.
  • Sharing wishes and resolutions for the coming year.

New Year's Eve is a festive celebration of hope, gratitude, and new beginnings in the Philippines.
